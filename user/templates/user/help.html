Introduction

Algorithmic trading uses a computer program that follows a defined set of instructions (an algorithm) to place a trade. The trade, in theory, can generate profits at a speed and frequency that is impossible for a human trader.

Use HODL to link your brokerage account and set up your first algorithmic trading !


Getting Start

Link to Brokerage

1. Sign up a trader account from Alpaca Market

2. Login your Alpaca Market account, find API keys and secret keys in your dashboard. (we currently only support paper trading account.)

3. Copy the API key and secret key of your Alpaca Market account into API Key page.


Set Up Algorithms

1. After you successfully link your Alpaca Market account, go to Algorithms page

2. Select any algorithm you want to use, click on Add Stock. Enter the stock symbol and parameters for the algorithm, then click Activate.

3. You're done! HODL will take care of your trading now!


Algorithms

Moving Average

Moving Average algorithm is based on calculating the moving average (MA) of a security's price over a specific period of time, such as the previous 50 days or 200 days. A moving average is a simple arithmetic average of the security's price over the specified period, and it is called "moving" because it is constantly recalculated as new data becomes available.

To set up a moving average algorithm, you need to input Short Term Moving Average and Long Term Moving Average. These are the numbers of days you want to calculate the average price.


Relative Strength Indicator

The RSI is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements in a security. Traders use the RSI to identify overbought and oversold conditions in the market.

To set up RSI, you need to input Moving Average Days, Over Percentage Threshold and Under Percentage Threshold.

Over Percentage Threshold and Under Percentage Threshold are numbers between 1 and 100.


Average True Range

Average True Range measures the average range of price movements in a security over a specified period of time. It takes into account any gaps in price movements and can provide a more accurate measure of volatility than other measures, such as standard deviation or variance.

To set up an average true range algorithm, you need to input Short Term Moving Average and Long Term Moving Average. These are the numbers of days you want to calculate the price movements.


MACD with Fibonacci

The MACD with Fibonacci trading algorithm is a technical analysis tool that combines two popular technical indicators: the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) and Fibonacci retracements.

The MACD is a momentum indicator that uses two exponential moving averages (EMA) of different periods to identify potential trend changes and momentum shifts in a security's price.

Fibonacci retracements are a set of horizontal lines drawn on a chart to indicate areas of support and resistance based on key Fibonacci ratios.

To set up an MACD algorithm, you need to input Short Term Moving Average and Long Term Moving Average. These are the numbers of days you want to calculate the moving average divergence.


Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands consist of a set of three bands, each of which is plotted numbers of standard deviations away from a simple moving average (SMA) of a security's price. The upper band represents the upper price level of the security, while the lower band represents the lower price level of the security. The middle band represents the average price level of the security. When the price of the security moves outside of the upper or lower bands, it may indicate a potential trend reversal or a breakout opportunity.

To set up an MACD algorithm, you need to input Days Moving Average and Standard Deviation. These are used for plotting the bands.
